Thanks for participating in this HIT qualifier! Please read the examples below, then complete the HIT (10 questions).

This qualifier will test your ability to label the relationship between sentences (called Natural Language Inference).

For each question, you will be given 2 sentences:

PremiseSentence describing a real-world situation, always assumed to be true.
Hypothesis An assumption or inference that we might make about the situation having read the premise.

There are 3 possible Relationships:

trueThe hypothesis is assumed or implied from the premise.
falseThe hypothesis is not assumed or implied from the premise. It contradicts the premise.
neitherThe hypothesis is possible from the premise, but not directly implied or contradicted. More context is needed.

For each question, your goal is to select the best Relationship.

Example #1

Premise: A person on a horse jumps over a broken down airplane.
Hypothesis: A person is at a diner, ordering an omelette.
What is the Relationship between the Premise and Hypothesis?

The correct answer is . Ordering food at a diner is not implied by jumping on a horse. In fact, it is contradictory: one of these events (on a horse) makes the other (at a diner) simultaneously impossible.
Example #2

Premise: A person on a horse jumps over a broken down airplane.
Hypothesis: A person is outdoors, on a horse.
What is the Relationship between the Premise and Hypothesis?

The correct answer is . It is assumed or implied by the first sentence that the person is outdoors in order to be jumping over an airplane.
Example #3

The correct answer is . The person may or may not be training for a competition. It is possible that they are, but is not directly implied and can't be concluded confidently.
