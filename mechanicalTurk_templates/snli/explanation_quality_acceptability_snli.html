Thanks for participating in this HIT!

You will read pairs of sentences that may or may not be related. For each pair of sentences, a label describing their relationship has been selected and 5 explanations have been given.

Your task is to judge whether each explanation is acceptable.


Each HIT contains 4 parts:

PremiseSentence describing a real-world situation, always assumed to be true.
Hypothesis An assumption or inference that we might make about the situation having read the premise.
RelationshipWhether the Hypothesis assumption or inference is valid given the Premise.
ExplanationsFive explanations for the Relationship.

A couple of notes:

  • You may disagree with the stated Relationship, but you should pretend it is correct when judging the Explanations.
  • Some sentences have minor grammar and spelling mistakes, don't end on a period, or cut off partway through because they are long. Please ignore these, and judge on the part of the sentence that is presented.
  • If at least part of the explanation is acceptable and sufficient (even if some other part is nonsensical or rambling), please mark is as acceptable.
Example #1:

Premise: A person on a horse jumps over a broken down airplane.
Hypothesis: A person is outdoors, on a horse.
Relationship: true
Which explanations are acceptable? Select all that you believe adequately explain the answer (this may be none).
Answer:


One possible answer is none of the above, because none of the explanations explain that a broken-down airplace is likely outdoors and that horse-riding is commonly done outdoors.

Another possible answer is to select only the last explanation as acceptable, since it does hint at the fact that being on a horse means being outdoors if the horse is outdoors.

Example #2:

Premise: Children smiling and waving at camera
Hypothesis: There are children present.
Relationship: true
Which explanations are acceptable? Select all that you believe adequately explain the answer (this may be none).
Answer:
